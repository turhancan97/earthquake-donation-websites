Helping Earthquake Victims in Turkey/Syria
On Feb. 6, 2023, two earthquakes with magnitudes of 7.7 and 7.5 hit 10 cities in Turkey.
The earthquakes in Turkey and Syria have had a devastating impact on many cities, thousands of families and millions of people and animals. These countries and their people need our support and assistance.
Figures show that more than 10,000 buildings have been destroyed, while an estimated 50,000 people are currently still trapped under the rubble. More than 13 million people were affected by the earthquakes, and the actual number of victims is estimated to be as high as 40,000. The situation is dire - Turkey has declared a three-month state of emergency in response to the disaster.
Rescue efforts are underway, but the search is being hampered by severe weather conditions, including snow, rain and extreme cold. Local services will spend the next days and months working to rebuild the city after the tragedy, but help from the international community is desperately needed right now .
If you would like to support earthquake victims, you can help in several ways. The following organizations are accepting donations to provide vital aid and support to those in need - we've divided the organizations into two groups below:

AKUT
It is an NGO specializing in the search, aid and rescue of earthquake victims. The organization is fully transparent about how it spends the donations it receives.
Union of Medical Care and Relief Organizations
An organization providing independent and impartial aid and medical care to victims of the war in Syria since 2012. It issued an urgent appeal to the international community to release emergency funds to provide immediate relief and rescue supplies.
UNHCR
UNHCR, the UN Refugee Agency, is a global organization dedicated to saving lives, protecting rights and building a better future for refugees and forcibly displaced communities.
TURKISH PHILANTHROPY FUNDS
Turkish Philanthropy Funds (TPF) is a leading U.S. foundation dedicated to high-impact social investment in Turkish and Turkish-American communities.
Kızılay
Turkey's largest blood donation organization. Currently stationed in the provinces of Adıyaman, Kilis, Şanlıurfa, Diyarbakır, Adana, Osmaniye, Gaziantep, Malatya and Hatay, providing the fulfillment of basic needs i.e. food, hygiene and shelter.
OXFAM
It is a global organization that fights inequality to end poverty and injustice, currently preparing to provide protection, water and sanitation, shelter and food support, and in the long term, rehabilitation and reconstruction.
AVAAZ
AVAAZ is a global online human rights movement. Currently, a group of volunteers is leading a rescue operation in Turkey, literally pulling people out from under the rubble with their bare hands. The rescuers are in need of medical equipment, fuel and temporary shelters - AVAAZ is donating the funds raised for these resources.
Time to Help
A volunteer group with a base in the UK that focuses on poverty and disaster relief. Funds will help provide shelter, food and medical assistance to those in need.
Siepomaga
Poland's largest online fundraising portal. Together with hundreds of thousands of our Helpers, they create a service that allows us to wake up better every day.
HELP FOR ANIMALS
HAYSEV / Angels Animal Sanctuary
Link 1, Link 2
The organization, which uses funds to help animals in the earthquake zone, buys food, shelter items (such as blankets) and medicines, among other things. It is under the Angels Animal Sanctuary organization, which has created an asylum for animals such as dogs, cats, but also cows, goats or birds used in the food animal industry or exploited by private individuals.

A collection to help rescue animals after the earthquake in Turkey, organized by the international organization fighting for the rights of all animals, Animal Save Movement. The Turkey branch of Animal Save is currently on the ground helping both human and non-human animals.
